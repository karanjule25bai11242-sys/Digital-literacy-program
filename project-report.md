## 🔐 Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

### 📖 Case Study – Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or bank details.  

In a typical scenario, a student receives an email that appears to be from a trusted bank. The email asks the user to click on a link and update their account information. The link leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. When the user enters their details, the attacker gains access to their account.  

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not verify such emails carefully. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and data misuse.  

---

### 🛡️ Prevention Checklist

- Always verify email senders before clicking links  
- Do not share OTPs or passwords with anyone  
- Use strong and unique passwords  
- Enable two-factor authentication  
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for transactions  
- Regularly update software and apps  
- Check website URLs carefully  
- Never click on suspicious links  

**📢 Report Cybercrime:**  
- Website: https://cybercrime.gov.in/  
- Helpline: 1930

While researching this topic, I was surprised at how easily people fall victim to phishing attacks. Even simple mistakes can lead to serious consequences. As a result, I will be more cautious while clicking links and sharing personal information online.
